Interesting facts about camel

1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

2.Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

3.Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting facts about chameleon

1.their feet work like salad tongs.

2.almost half of all known species live in madagascar

3.chameleon vary wildly in size.

Interesting facts about cicada

1.cicada are physically distinguished by their stout bodies, broad heads, clear-membraned wings, and large compound eyes.

2.there are more than 3000 species of cicada.

3. periodical cicadas occur most years in different geographic regions

Interesting facts about panda

1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

2.Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

3.An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting facts about panda

1.Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

2.Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

3.Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years.

Interesting facts about teddy bear

1.The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

2.The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.

3.In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
